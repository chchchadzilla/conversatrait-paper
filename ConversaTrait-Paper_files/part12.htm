B. Cognitive and Emotional Trait Inference‌

ConversaTrait’s analytical framework determines cognitive abilities and emotional intelli- gence from linguistic patterns, forming a core component of its “Algorithmic Behavioral Fingerprint” system. For cognitive abilities, the system analyzes syntactic complexity, in- cluding metrics such as sentence length, clause embedding, and the frequency of complex grammatical structures. These features indicate advanced thought processes, reflecting an individual’s capacity for intricate mental operations and sophisticated articulation 20. Lexical diversity, quantified by measures like Type-Token Ratio and uncommon vocabulary, gauges verbal fluency and intellectual breadth, indicating a rich internal lexicon and cog- nitive flexibility 21. The system also scrutinizes topic shifts and discourse markers—such as “therefore” or “however”—to assess logical reasoning and coherence, reflecting an in- dividual’s capacity for structured thought and effective argumentation 22. These linguistic indicators, inherent to the “linguistic unconscious”, provide objective markers of cognitive function.

Emotional intelligence inference involves examining emotional valence through sentiment analysis across utterances. This process identifies emotions and their nuanced expression and regulation, revealing underlying emotional states that individuals may not consciously acknowledge 23. Pragmatic cues, encompassing indirect speech acts, politeness markers, and empathy indicators, reveal an individual’s ability to understand and navigate complex social contexts 24. These cues are crucial for understanding interpersonal dynamics and social acumen, establishing a foundation for advanced emotional assessment.
