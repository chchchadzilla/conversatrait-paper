IV.A. User Consent and Data Sovereignty‌


ConversaTrait’s ethical framework is built upon the requirement of explicit, informed, and granular consent from the data subject for any conversational data analysis. This commitment to user autonomy extends beyond regulatory compliance, establishing a foundational principle of Privacy by Design (Local Data Sovereignty). ConversaTrait employs a multi-stage consent process, ensuring users genuinely understand and actively participate in the data analysis.

Upon initial engagement or when selecting new data sources, users encounter clear, unambiguous prompts. These prompts outline the precise data type undergoing anal- ysis, the explicit purpose of this analysis, the specific psychological and personality traits inferred, and the stringent privacy protocols governing data handling. This ap- proach mandates an active opt-in. Users must explicitly confirm their consent by ticking boxes or clicking “Agree” after reviewing the terms. To facilitate comprehen- sion, interactive educational overlays explain complex terminology, such as “linguis- tic unconscious” or “ephemeral data,” in plain language, ensuring users grasp the implications of their consent.

A core aspect of user control is the explicit right to revoke consent at any time. Users receive clear instructions on how to delete all locally stored data and cease future analysis, reinforcing their ultimate ownership of sensitive information. Con- versaTrait’s local data storage architecture avoids centralized Personally Identifiable Information (PII) storage. This design positions consent as a mechanism for local pro- cessing and anonymized feature extraction for LLM inference, rather than for data col- lection by ConversaTrait [?]. Raw, identifiable conversational data remains within the user’s local environment. Conversations involving multiple participants present a unique ethical consideration. ConversaTrait addresses this with a stringent default setting: co-conversationalists are anonymized, and reports are generated exclusively for the primary data subject. Accessing specific insights about co-conversationalists requires a separate, explicit, and informed consent mechanism from each individual. This process involves a se- cure, verifiable method to obtain their consent, with clear disclosure regarding the scope of analysis and data handling. This methodology adheres to data protection standards such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)34 and ensures eth- ical data usage, preventing unauthorized profiling of third parties. This comprehen- sive approach to consent reinforces user control over their sensitive data, ensuring personal information remains solely within their dominion, thereby contributing to an Ethical Trust Framework and fostering Augmented Professional Efficacy through trust and transparency.
